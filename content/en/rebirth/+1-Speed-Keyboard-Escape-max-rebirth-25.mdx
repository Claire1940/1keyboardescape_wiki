<Section icon={Map} title="Keyboard Worlds and Stage Obstacles">

Reaching the end of World 1 and World 2 requires navigating tricky obstacles. While speed is helpful, timing and route knowledge are essential to prevent falling and resetting your progress. Follow these steps to navigate the most difficult stages.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prepare Before Pushing">
    Before entering new stage chains, stack rebirths, your best trail, your best aura, and treadmill speed. Entering underleveled makes simple jumps feel inconsistent and wastes repeated attempts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Control Speed on Parkour">
    At around 100 speed, the parkour on World 1 Stage 4 can be skipped with a strong jump. At very high speed, run straight through instead of overcorrecting jumps, as too much speed can make short jumps harder to control.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Time the Hazards">
    For the chocolate tsunami on Stage 6, wait until the wave is right in front of you, then move after it passes. For Stage 14, go as soon as the faster wave appears. Hesitating on Stage 14 misses the timing window.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Memorize Maze Routes">
    In the Stage 12 maze, take the first right, keep following the path, turn right at the first intersection, then take the next right again. Route memory makes late World 1 clears much more stable.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Important Info">
If you struggle with jump controls at high speeds, try toggling your walk speed or practicing short taps instead of holding the jump key.
</Callout>
